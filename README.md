# Customer Churn Analysis: Identifying Why Customers Leave

## Business Problem

Customer churn is one of the biggest threats to business growth.  
Acquiring new customers is expensive — retaining existing ones is far more profitable.

In this project, I analyzed customer behavior to answer a key question:

**Why are customers leaving, and what can be done to stop it?**

---

## Objective

- Identify the main drivers of customer churn  
- Understand how customer value impacts retention  
- Provide actionable, business-focused recommendations  

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Churn is critically high

**35.4% of customers are leaving**, indicating a clear retention issue.

---

### 2. Membership tier is the strongest retention driver

- Bronze: **51.7% churn**  
- Silver: **49.6% churn**  
- Gold: **5.1% churn**  

**High-value customers stay, while low-tier customers are at high risk.**

---

### 3. Low spending = high churn risk

- Churned customers spend approximately **36% less**  

Customer value is directly linked to retention.

---

### 4. Engagement does not guarantee retention

Some customers show high purchase activity but still churn.

Short-term engagement does not ensure long-term loyalty.

---

### 5. Discount strategy requires review

Customers without discounts show lower churn.

This may indicate inefficient targeting or dependency effects.

---

### 6. Satisfaction alone is not enough

- Weak predictor when analyzed alone  
- Strong when combined with behavioral variables  

Context is more important than isolated metrics.

---

## Business Impact

This analysis reveals a clear pattern:

> Churn is concentrated among low-value and low-engagement customers.

Addressing this issue could:

- Increase revenue  
- Reduce customer acquisition costs  
- Improve long-term customer lifetime value  

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Promote membership upgrades

Encourage customers to move to higher tiers through incentives and added value.

---

### 2. Act early on low-value customers

Identify and engage customers before they disengage.

---

### 3. Improve post-purchase retention

- Loyalty programs  
- Follow-ups  
- Personalized offers  

---

### 4. Optimize discount strategies

Focus on targeted discounts instead of general usage.

---

### 5. Use behavioral data

Combine multiple variables to understand better and predict churn.

---

## Predictive Model

To complement the analysis, a model was developed to predict churn risk.

### Model

- Logistic Regression  

### Performance

- Accuracy: **0.98**  
- Recall: **1.00**  
- F1 Score: **0.98**  

The model successfully identifies all churned customers, prioritizing recall, which is critical in retention strategies.
